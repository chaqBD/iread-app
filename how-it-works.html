The Process

How iRead Works

Each month follows a clear democratic process — from nomination to live discussion and beyond. Here is everything from the four-step cycle to the 13-dimension review framework.

Monthly Cycle

The four-step reading cycle

Each month follows a clear democratic process — from nomination to live discussion and beyond.

01
📚

Nominate

Members nominate one article each via Google Form. Must include full citation, ABS ranking, and a 2-sentence rationale.

02
🗳️

Vote

A curated shortlist of 5 goes to community vote via WhatsApp and LinkedIn polls. Top 3 articles are selected.

03
🎙️

Listen

A podcast episode drops mid-month: 20 minutes covering aims, methodology, findings, gaps, and policy implications.

04
💬

Debate

Last Thursday of the month: a 90-minute live virtual session. Reviewers present. Community debates. Gaps are mapped.

Session Format

90 minutes of pure intellectual engagement

Not a seminar. Not a lecture. A structured, energetic debate where every voice matters and every gap spotted is a potential PhD contribution.

Reading Framework

The 13-Dimension Review Framework

Every article reviewed through the same rigorous lens. Two volunteer reviewers prepare this before each session.

01
Bibliographic Details
Authors, year, journal, ABS ranking, DOI, citation count.
02
Aims & Objectives
What does the paper set out to do? Are the aims clearly stated and achieved?
03
Research Questions
What questions/hypotheses are addressed? Are they novel and well-grounded?
04
Theoretical Framework
Which theories/models are used? Is the theoretical grounding appropriate?
05
Methodology
Research design, data collection, sample, analytical technique — all justified?
06
Key Findings
Main results — what is surprising, expected, or contested?
07
Theoretical Contributions
Does this extend, challenge, or merely replicate existing theory?
08
Practical Contributions
What do managers and leaders gain? Is it actionable?
09
Policy Implications
What does this suggest for governments, regulators, institutions — UK or global?
10
Strengths
What does this paper do particularly well — rigour, originality, clarity?
11
Weaknesses & Limitations
Blind spots, methodological flaws, generalisation issues, potential bias?
12
Research Gaps
What future research is called for? What PhD-worthy gaps do YOU spot?
13
Community Verdict
Score 1–10. Must-read or skip? Would you cite it? Group consensus vs. dissent — all documented.
